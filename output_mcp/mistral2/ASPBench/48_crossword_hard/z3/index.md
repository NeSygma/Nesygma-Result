#### Entities and Variables
1. **Grid**: A 6x6 grid with black squares at the corners: (0,0), (0,5), (5,0), (5,5). The rest are initially empty.
2. **Words**: 8 three-letter words: CAT, ACE, TEA, EAR, ATE, RAT, CAR, TAR.
3. **Placements**: Each word must be placed exactly once, either horizontally or vertically.
   - For horizontal placements: `(row, col)` is the starting position, and the word occupies `(row, col)` to `(row, col+2)`.
   - For vertical placements: `(row, col)` is the starting position, and the word occupies `(row, col)` to `(row+2, col)`.
4. **Constraints**:
   - **Grid Bounds**: Words must fit within the grid (no out-of-bounds placements).
   - **Black Squares**: Words cannot start on or cross through black squares.
   - **Word Integrity**: Each word is placed exactly once.
   - **No Conflicts**: No two placements can assign different letters to the same cell.
   - **Intersections**: At least 3 intersection points where horizontal and vertical words share a cell.
   - **Letter Matching**: At intersections, both words must have the same letter.
   - **Connectivity**: All placed letters must form a single connected component (no isolated words).

#### Approach
- Represent the grid as a 6x6 array of `String` or `Char` variables, with black squares fixed as `'#'` and other cells initially empty (`' '`).
- Use a list of placements, where each placement is a tuple `(word, row, col, direction)`.
- Ensure each word is placed exactly once.
- Enforce constraints:
  - No placement starts or crosses a black square.
  - No conflicts in cell assignments.
  - At least 3 intersections with matching letters.
  - All placed letters are connected (using a graph-based connectivity check).

#### Z3 Representation
- Use `String` or `Char` for grid cells.
- Use `Int` for row and column indices.
- Use `Bool` to track placements and intersections.
- Use `Solver` to enforce constraints.
